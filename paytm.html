Overview

Making wealth management feel personal.

Paytm Money is India's leading digital investment platform — a wholly-owned subsidiary of Paytm. When I joined, the product was early-stage and ambitious. When I left six years later, over a million Indians were trusting it with their savings.

The fundamental design challenge never changed: how do you make genuinely complex financial products — fund selection, SIP setup, KYC compliance, portfolio tracking — feel approachable without dumbing them down? The answer lives in the details: copy that educates without intimidating, progressive disclosure that respects user time, and onboarding flows that earn trust step by step.

The Problem

KYC was silently killing the funnel.

When I audited the onboarding funnel, over 60% of drop-offs happened during KYC — the identity verification step every SEBI-regulated platform requires. Users were confused, scared, and calling support in droves. The existing flow had 11 steps, no progress indicators, legal language no normal person understands, and error messages shown only at the end of the form.

I redesigned the entire flow — 11 screens became 6, plain-language copy replaced legal jargon, real-time field validation replaced batch errors, and a persistent step indicator gave users control. Support queries related to KYC fell 70% within two months of launch.

Design System

Building the foundation once.

I led the creation of Paytm Money's full design system — design tokens, colour semantics, typography scale, component states, dark/light mode parity, and accessibility specs. This scaled the design team from 2 to 8 designers without sacrificing quality. Handoff time per feature dropped by 40%.

Overview

One app. Everything.

Before "super app" was even a word in India, Tapzo was building one. Travel bookings, food delivery, mobile recharge, movie tickets, and utility bill payments — all unified under a single, seamless experience. I joined as a Product Designer in 2014 and was part of the core team that shipped the product through to its Amazon acquisition in 2017.

The fundamental design challenge: six completely different service verticals, each with their own data models, user expectations, and visual languages — made to feel like one coherent product. Consistency without monotony.

Design Challenge

Consistency without monotony.

The hardest part of designing a super app isn't any single vertical — it's the transitions between them. A user booking a flight has entirely different mental models than a user ordering a pizza. Making both feel natural within the same shell required a foundational design system built specifically for contextual switching.

I built a shared navigation and interaction language that users could learn once and apply everywhere. Service-specific colour accents helped users orient instantly without breaking the unified feel. The search bar became the unifying product moment — a single gesture that worked across all six verticals.
